Architecture

Three Engines, One Decision

ADAPT is built on three interacting components — two independent signal engines and a dynamic allocator that reads their combined output to determine portfolio positioning in real time.

C

ADAPT Core

Primary Regime Classifier

The Core engine analyzes market structure and classifies the current environment into one of four regimes — from aggressive re-entry on extreme oversold conditions, through momentum participation with partial protection, to a full defensive multi-asset basket. The strategy spends over 81% of its time in capital preservation mode, deploying aggressively only when conditions structurally justify it. This asymmetry is intentional and is the source of ADAPT's drawdown control.

A

ADAPT Alpha

Independent Confirmation Layer

The Alpha engine operates on entirely different logic from Core, providing a second opinion that either confirms or contradicts the Core regime signal. It is specifically designed to catch momentum inflections that Core, being structurally conservative, may be slower to recognize. The two engines are intentionally complementary — not redundant.

⟁

COMBINED

Dynamic Allocator — The Default Strategy

COMBINED is the final decision engine and the recommended default for all users. It reads the outputs from both Core and Alpha, determines whether they agree or disagree, and sets portfolio allocation accordingly. The result is a system that is aggressive when both signals align bullish, cautious when they disagree, and maximally defensive when both signal risk-off. The portfolio breathes with the market rather than applying static rules.

The Allocator

Why COMBINED Is the Right Engine

During development, both Core-only and Alpha-only configurations were evaluated. Each had meaningful weaknesses in isolation — Core alone is slow to re-enter fast momentum reversals, while Alpha alone lacks the regime-classification depth needed during extended bear markets. COMBINED captures the interaction between the two, producing a result neither achieves alone.

Validation

Four Layers of Verification

ADAPT was subjected to a comprehensive multi-stage audit process. The strategy failed its first audit (look-ahead bias discovered), was fully restructured, and then passed an independent re-audit with all tests clean. Every metric shown on this page reflects the post-audit, fully validated system.

🔍

Look-Ahead Bias Audit

Verdict: Clean ✓

A line-by-line code audit confirmed that signal generation uses only data available at market close on the signal date. Active weights always lag one full bar. The circuit breaker is fully stateful with no future data. Transaction costs are applied consistently on every weight change. No look-ahead bias detected at any stage.

📊

Walk-Forward Testing

Verdict: Robust ✓

The gold standard for detecting overfitting. Using a rolling 3-year in-sample / 1-year out-of-sample structure: mean IS CAGR of 27.56% vs mean OOS CAGR of 27.08%. A near-perfect match is the strongest possible evidence against overfitting. 10/10 out-of-sample windows were positive.

🎯

Parameter Sensitivity

Verdict: Stable Plateau ✓

Each key parameter was varied independently while holding all others fixed. Most parameters sit on a stable plateau — performance degrades gradually rather than falling off a cliff. The strategy is not balanced on a knife-edge of precisely tuned values. It occupies a robust, repeatable region of parameter space.

🎲

Monte Carlo Simulation

Verdict: Genuine Repeatable Edge ✓

10,000 simulated paths using block bootstrap resampling (20-day blocks) tested whether performance depends on a lucky historical sequence. Actual CAGR: 26.66%. Monte Carlo 5-year median: 26.94%. The strategy's edge is structural — not dependent on specific market dates or sequences.

Development Discipline

Built to Last, Not to Impress

ADAPT was developed under a strict set of principles designed to prevent the most common failure modes in systematic strategy development. Every parameter in the system earned its place through structural justification — not data mining.

01

Incremental improvements only — one change at a time, never compound modifications that obscure causality.

02

Structural justification required before any parameter is tested. No data mining. No fishing for patterns that look good in hindsight.

03

Out-of-sample validation before adoption — no parameter is permanent until it passes on data the system has never seen.

04

Walk-forward positive windows is the deciding criterion — not raw CAGR. Robustness above returns.

05

Thresholds are never optimized for performance. Only statistical or structural reasoning is accepted for any parameter choice.
